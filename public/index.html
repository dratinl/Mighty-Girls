The Mighty Girls

Who are the Mighty Girls?

The Mighty Girls are a youth soccer club started in Fall 2017 and are affiliated with the SSF United Youth Soccer League. We are a recreational team focused on building skills and having fun. We run practice from 4:30 - 5:30 PM at Orange Park in South San Francisco on Tuesdays and Thursdays with games on Saturday Morning at the same location.

How can I sign my child up?

Excited to hear your interest! First, head to the SSF United registration hub. There you will find detailed instructions on how to sign your child up for the league.

Next, make sure you have all of your forms (3) signed and filled out. You will also need your child's Proof of Birth, a digital photo and a $120 fee.

Once you have all the paperwork in order you can go ahead and e-mail that to the league. Cash or Check payments can be sent to the addresss listed on the SSF United page.

Remember to include The Mighty Girls, Coach Edwin or Coach Jacob on the 1601 form in the section shown below. If you find yourself having any problems with the registration process please to reach out via the contact options found at the bottom of the page!

Meet the Coaches

Coach Edwin Arriola

Coach Edwin has been coaching youth soccer teams since 1997 and has always been an advocate of giving every individual ample play time. Coach Edwin brings a focus on teamplay, respect and learning the fundamentals of the sport while keeping it fun and engaging.

Coach Jacob Arriola

As a South San Francisco native, Jacob grew up playing in soccer in the SSF area. He believes heavily in learning from mistakes and teaching children how to find answers for themselves. Coach Jacob brings a focus on technical skills and thinking outside of the box.

This section is dedicated to breaking down what the kids are learning.

Fundamentals

Passing

Currently we teach two different methods of passing. The first method is passing with the inside of the foot and focuses on control and accuracy. The second method is passing with the shoelaces which focuses on power and distance.

Dribbling

The Mighty Girls focus on learning to dribble with control and awareness. We teach the players to dribble with the top of their foot with their heads up.

Shooting

The technique for shooting at this stage of development is very similar to that of passing with power. The focus here is to develop confidence in the player's ability to shoot.

Skills

Here we have a list of skills we are currently teaching the players.

Current:

  • Pull Back
  • Step Over / Scissor Kick
  • Pull and Flick
  • Cut

Planned:

  • Roulette
  • Fake shot

Homework!

The current homework for The Mighty Girls is to practice juggling! The secret to juggling is to keep the foot horizontal and kick the ball with the shoelaces. Juggling is one of the best (and definitely one of the most fun) ways to teach individuals the feel of the ball, which translates to improved handling, kicking and dribbling.
